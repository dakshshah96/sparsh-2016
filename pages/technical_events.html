1. Autobot

A factory has caught fire and the whole building has collapsed. The surroundings are covered completely in smoke. Some of the workers are stuck inside the factory and fireman trying to go inside the building to save them can't see anything due to the smoke and flames. So, you have to design a self-controlled bot which stops on detecting the fire and helps fireman to get the workers to safety. So guys, wire up your bots because it’s time for some real action!

The event consists of two rounds. All the contestants will participate in the first round. The first round serves to be a qualifying round and the qualifying contestants are allowed to participate in the second round.

  • Rounds
    • Round 1
    • Round 2
  • Robot Specifications
  • Team Specifications
  • Scoring
  • Rules

Simple Line Follower

  • The base of the arena is made up of white colored flex sheet with a black colored line (to be followed) on it.
  • The thickness of the line will be 3 cm.
  • The course line may have acute, obtuse and right angle curves.
  • Organizers decision shall be treated as final and binding on all.

Stop and Go

  • The base of the arena is made up of white colored flex sheet with a black colored line (to be followed) on it.
  • The thickness of the line will be 3 cm.
  • The course line may have acute, obtuse and right angle curves.
  • There will be a light sources (check points) fixed at different points on the arena. The bot should stop at the point where there is maximum intensity of the light source as long as it is on and once the light turns off, the bot should resume its path and should reach the end point.
  • Organizers decision shall be treated as final and binding on all.
  • The robot should fit inside a cube of side 20-25 cm.
  • Robot should be completely autonomous.
  • The robot can be both internally or externally powered.
  • The power supply at any point on robot should not be greater than 20V.
  • A tolerance of 5% is allowed in the dimensions.
  • No major change in bot will be allowed after successful registration.

A team can consist of maximum four members. Individuals may also participate.

Scoring will vary according to the round and the same shall be communicated to the contestants before the start of each round.

  • Event Managers and Coordinators reserve the right to ask for explanation about the robot at any time during the event.
  • Winner of competition is the one who successfully reaches the end point after crossing all the check points.
  • The robot should follow the line accurately. If the robot deviates from the line, contestants are allowed to keep the bot manually in its right direction {HAND TOUCH}
  • Only one restart is allowed in each round.
  • A sample arena will be available for calibration and the final arena will be disclosed only after starting the rounds.
  • The judgment of organizers is final and abiding to all.
  • We reserve the right at any time and in our sole discretion to make changes to rules and regulations without prior notice.
  • Between trails and events, participants should not feed information about the arena to the bot.
  • In case of any disputes the organizers decision will be final and binding.
  • The participants are requested no to assume anything without conforming it from the event Organizers.
  • Innovative ideas are welcomed and separate awards may be given for the best design of bot, best bot, etc.

2. Wall-E

The participants have to build an autonomous robot which is able to solve a wall maze in minimum possible time. The bot has to follow the path as directed by the walls of the arena until the end of the maze.

  • Arena Specifications
  • Game Play
  • Robot Specifications

The arena consist of a real wall maze, in which walls are made of wood/plastic. The distance between the walls is approx 32 cm. The robot has to sense the walls and move forward into the maze. The arena consists of only right turns and the dead end. The height of the walls is 20 cm.

The bot has to start from the starting point and follow the path in the wall maze. Points are given for each check point the bot has crossed. Negative points will be awarded for hand touches and if the bot touches the wall. The time taken by the bot to complete the maze is considered in the overall points the bot has collected. The ranking of the participants will be done based on the total points acquired by the team.

  • Dimension: 25cm X 25cm
  • Control: Autonomous
  • Power Supply: Battery (24V), Wireless
  • Hint: TSop, Proximity Sensor, Ultrasonic Sensors etc. can be used for sensing the walls.

3. Line Follower

The participants have to build an autonomous robot which is able to follow a black line on a white arena. The bot has to solve a specific task in minimum possible time.

  • Arena Specifications
  • Game Play
  • Robot Specifications
  • The arena consists of black line on white background. The bot has to follow the black line without deviation. The width of the black line is 3cm. The arena consist of two parts.
  • The first part of the contest consists of a 6*6 grid. The bot has to search for a white node in this 6*6 grid by moving through each and every node of the grid. When the bot finds the node, it has to remember the coordinates of that node and then stop searching in the grid and move towards to the second part of the arena.
  • The second part of the contest consists of a maze made of the same black lines over white background as in the first part. If the coordinates of the first node are (x,y), the key to solve the maze is x right turns and y left turns.
  • The bot has to take x right turns and y left turns alternatively to reach the end of part two.
  • First the robot has to enter the first part from start and start searching for the white node in the grid. The points were given for the successful completion of the first and second part.
  • First the bot has to take a dry run through the whole arena i.e. 1st and 2nd parts. At the completion of the second part, the bot has to save the whole path followed i.e., the coordinates in 1st part and the path to solve the maze in the 2nd part.
  • Then the bot has to follow the whole arena based on the memory saved during the dry run, this is the memory run.
  • The ranking is done on the points acquired by the team and the time taken during the dry run and the memory run.
  • Dimension: 25cm X 25cm
  • Control: Autonomous
  • Power Supply: Battery (24V), Wireless
  • Hint: LED/LDR, IR Sensor, Color Sensors etc. can be used for sensing the lines.

12. 2 X 10 Relay

  • Teamwork divides the task and multiplies the success.
  • Caution: This time choose your team wisely!
  • It’s a relay, each one’s ability matters, and no discussion. The race is set. Accuracy and speed of both the team coders matters.
  • Code the problem in C/C++ that executes in the stipulated time.
  • Rules:
    • Team of two.
    • No communication will be allowed between two members.
    • Each member will code for 10 minutes, and then swapped.
    • No external help will be allowed.
    • The decision of judges will be final.

Rules:

  • A problem statement would be given, and you have to code it with the monitor switched off.
  • The decision of judges will be final.

15. MindF Quiz

Which is better, eternal happiness or a ham sandwich?

It would appear that eternal happiness is better, but this is really not so! After all, nothing is better than eternal happiness, and a ham sandwich is certainly better than nothing. Therefore a ham sandwich is better than eternal happiness.

If such puzzles fascinate you, then here is your chance to put your brain against some of the very logical puzzles and code.

Rules:

  • Team of two.
  • No external help is allowed.
  • Solve the given logical and basic C programming questions.
  • Bring your brain(s) and a pen.
  • The decision of judges will be final.
